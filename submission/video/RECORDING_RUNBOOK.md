# Recording Runbook

Follow this runbook from top to bottom. It assumes screen recording and voice
recording can be captured separately and combined in any ordinary video
editor.

## 1. Prepare once

1. Use a desktop browser at `1920 × 1080` or larger with browser zoom at
   `100%`.
2. Set the recording canvas and final export to `1920 × 1080`, `30 fps`, and
   `16:9`.
3. Turn off operating-system notifications and close unrelated tabs.
4. Use the deployed app: <https://yo4e.github.io/maybe-tomorrow/>.
5. Follow [`RESET_AND_FALLBACK.md`](./RESET_AND_FALLBACK.md) to clear the
   visible journal and reload session-only sample state.
6. Open [`ANSWER_KEYS.md`](./ANSWER_KEYS.md) on a second device or print it; do
   not place it over the recorded product.
7. Rehearse each clip once without recording. Confirm the exact result before
   the real take.
8. Prepare a quiet microphone. Headphones are optional because the product has
   no required audio.

## 2. Record silent product clips

Record each item as a separate file. Leave one second of stillness before and
after every clip for editing handles.

### Clip 1 — Home

1. Reload the clean app.
2. Keep the cursor away from the headline.
3. Hold on **Does this really have to happen today?**, the empty activity
   field, and **Check one thing** for at least five seconds.

### Clip 2 — Quick Check

1. Enter `Start another side project`.
2. Select **Check one thing**.
3. Complete the eight core answers in [`ANSWER_KEYS.md`](./ANSWER_KEYS.md).
4. For each question, pause just long enough for the selected visible label to
   register. Record the complete flow; cut cursor travel later.

### Clip 3 — Verdict and Cost of Yes

1. Begin on the completed **Maybe tomorrow.** result.
2. Hold on the verdict and leading factors.
3. Select **What would need to change?**.
4. Scroll slowly enough to reveal the nearest alternative facts and the
   `78,125 combinations` proof.

### Clip 4 — Sample Today Map

1. Return home.
2. Select **Try the sample day**.
3. Pause on the already selected human labels.
4. Select **View this day**.
5. Hold on the four metrics: `11 hr 30 min`, `30 min`, `0 min`, and
   `1 hr 15 min`.
6. Scroll the fictional event list once. Do not suggest that an overlap exists.

### Clip 5 — Room-making preview

1. From Today Map, select **Check one thing**.
2. Enter `Draft another short story`, choose **1 hr**, and continue.
3. Complete the sample answers from [`ANSWER_KEYS.md`](./ANSWER_KEYS.md).
4. Hold on **Maybe tomorrow.**.
5. Scroll to the already visible **See what would have to move** section.
6. Hold on the plan that shortens **Go to the gym** by `30 min`, leaves
   `5:00–6:00 PM` open, and reports `18 allowed combinations`.
7. Do not select or save the plan; this is a preview.

### Clip 6 — Collaboration evidence

Record tight, readable crops of public repository evidence. Either use the
public GitHub pages or local Markdown/history views with unrelated UI cropped
out.

1. signed specification files in `docs/`;
2. Issue #3 proposal and Templex approval;
3. Issue #5 Yoshie feedback and Templex approval;
4. Codex commits and merged Pull Request #4; and
5. `CODEX_WORKLOG.md` showing the final 10 files / 71 tests verification.

Do not show notifications, private account information, personal browser
history, or unrelated repositories.

### Clip 7 — Closing

1. Return to the deployed app.
2. Hold on the product name and `No AI reads your calendar. We can see the
   time. Only you know what it means.`
3. End without moving the cursor for at least two seconds.

## 3. Record narration

1. Open [`TELEPROMPTER.txt`](./TELEPROMPTER.txt).
2. Record its seven paragraphs as seven separate audio takes.
3. Read conversationally, not as an advertisement.
4. Aim for the caption segment lengths in [`SHOT_LIST.md`](./SHOT_LIST.md).
5. If a paragraph runs long, rerecord it rather than speeding up the audio.
6. Pronounce `ICS` as the letters “I-C-S,” `ZIP` as “zip,” and `GPT-5.6 Sol`
   as “G-P-T five point six Sol.”
7. Keep natural breaths between sentences, then remove only distracting dead
   air.

The locked script is `283` words. At 115 words per minute it is approximately
`2:28`, leaving more than 30 seconds below the official three-minute maximum.

## 4. Assemble to the locked timeline

1. Create a `2:30` timeline using [`SHOT_LIST.md`](./SHOT_LIST.md).
2. Place narration first, then fit the corresponding screen clips below it.
3. Use straight cuts or very short dissolves only.
4. Prefer a still frame over sped-up unreadable controls.
5. Add [`CAPTIONS.srt`](./CAPTIONS.srt) as burned-in captions or as the YouTube
   caption track. If burned in, keep two lines maximum and avoid covering
   controls.
6. Use no music. Product silence under narration is intentional.
7. End by `2:30`; never add an outro that pushes the export to `3:00` or more.

## 5. Export and verify

1. Export MP4 using H.264 video and AAC audio at `1920 × 1080`.
2. Play the complete local export with headphones.
3. Complete every item in [`QA_CHECKLIST.md`](./QA_CHECKLIST.md).
4. Upload to YouTube using [`YOUTUBE_METADATA.md`](./YOUTUBE_METADATA.md).
5. Set visibility to **Public** in YouTube Studio.
6. Open the final URL in a signed-out/private browser window and play from
   beginning to end.
